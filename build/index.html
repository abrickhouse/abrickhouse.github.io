HackBeanpot: Bostonian


Bostonian is an iOS app for young people in Boston looking for fun things to do. Image Processing Application


This was a class project for Object-Oriented Design. The application allows the user to load in images from their computer, manipulate them, and then save the image under a new name. It can handle jpg, png, bmp, and ppm file types. The application has many capabilities: filtering, different kinds of greyscale, brightening/darkening, flipping vertically or horizontally, and downscaling to name a few. We made both a GUI and text-based interface. It was coded in Java.

Marble Solitaire Game


This was a class project for Object-Oriented Design. We programmed the marble soliatire game (English, European, and Triangular versions). Marble Solitaire is a strategy game in which the player tries to leave as few marbles on the board as possible by jumping marbles pieces into empty spaces and removing jumped marbles. For this project, we focused on Model-View-Controller pattern and other design patterns. We made both a GUI and text-based interface. It was coded in Java.

Maze


This was a class project for Fundamentals of CS 2. The program randomly generates a mazes by creating a graph where the edges have randomly generated weights and then finding a minimum spanning tree using kruskal’s algorithm. The user can then press 'b' to run a breadth-first search or 'd' to run a depth-first search until a path to the exit-point at the bottom right corner is found. They can then press 'r' to reset and create a new maze. It was coded in Java.

Printed Brochure

An Informational Climate Change brochure.

In this project, I chose to create an informative brochure on saving the environment. Specifically, my brochure regarded a recent report put out by the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change. The project was designed to spread awareness about the quickly closing window of opportunity to deal with the climate crisis. I was drawn to this topic because it not only concerns protecting the planet but also examines the vulnerable groups of people that are in danger if nothing is done. After picking out the information that I wanted to include from the report, I sketched out a rough draft that showed where I planned to put certain text and images. Throughout the process, I was especially focused on typography. I was concerned with the text hierarchy on the front page, as well as, the alignment, sizing, and spaces of words and paragraphs on the information-dense inner pages. I adjusted the sizing of the elements until it felt like they were neither fighting for space nor drowning in it. It was important to me that the brochure felt cohesive. In line with this goal, I strived to make the color schemes and fonts emphasize the central themes of nature and urgency. The final project was a six page printed brochure made in Adobe InDesign.

Patterned Sweatshirt

A Yayoi Kusama inspired patterned sweatshirt.

I was tasked with creating a piece of art inspired by the Japanese artist Yayoi Kusama. For this project, I looked into Kusama’s history, processes, and artworks. I was particularly intrigued by her fascination with sprawling patterns, warping the viewer’s field of vision like an illusion. Following my research into her, I decided to bleach a black sweatshirt with a design derived from Kusama’s famous polka dot obsession. I feel that, by mirroring her process and painstakingly detailing each individual spot, I was able to gain insight into her perspective. Channeling Yayoi Kusama’s methodology allowed me to tap into my own ideas of organized chaos and infinity. By drawing each circle in white colored pencil and then painting each individually, I covered the shirt completely with my design, leaving no clear end or beginning. I was interested in exploring the use of shapes and their relation to one another to convey an infinite design. This piece is my visual interpretation of the mind-warping effect that Kusama’s art had on people.
